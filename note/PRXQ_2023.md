## Singular-value statistics of non-Hermitian random matrix

### A. Singular-value statistics

The singular values of matrix ${ H }$ are the **square roots of eigenvalues** of ${ H^{\dagger} H }$ or ${ HH^{\dagger} }$.

Singular values are always **real and non-negative** number.

The **singular value spacing ratio** is defined as
$$
\begin{align*}
  r_{n}
  \equiv 
  \min( \frac{s_{n + 1} - s_{n}}{s_{n}- s_{n - 1}}, \frac{s_{n}- s_{n - 1}}{s_{n + 1}- s_{n}} ),\quad r_{n} \in [0, 1]
\end{align*}
$$
- ${ n  }$-th **ordered** singular value: ${ s_{n} }$ 

Such distribution of ${ r_{n} }$ don't require the **unfolding** operation.

### B. Hermitization

Introducing the Hermitization as
$$
\begin{align*}
  \tilde{H}
  \equiv 
  
  \begin{bmatrix}
    0 & H \\
    H^{\dagger}  & 0 \\
  \end{bmatrix}
\end{align*}
$$
- non-Hermitian matrix: ${ H }$ 

The singular values of ${ \tilde{H} }$ are given by the eigenvalues of ${ \sqrt{H^{\dagger}H} }$ and ${ \sqrt{HH^{\dagger}} }$.

The singular values of ${ H }$ are given by the eigenvalues of ${ \sqrt{H^{\dagger}H} }$ or ${ \sqrt{HH^{\dagger}} }$.

Therefore, the singular values of Hermitian ${ \tilde{H} }$ are two-times copy of those of ${ H }$.


## Dissipative quantum chaos

We will show that non-integrable open quantum system obey the statistics of non-Hermitian random matrix.

### A. Lindbladians

<!-- master equation -->
The master equation reads
$$
\begin{align*}
  \frac{\mathrm{d}\rho}{\mathrm{d}t}
  &=
  - \mathrm{i} [H, \rho] + \sum_{n} \left( L_{n}\rho L_{n}^{\dagger} - \frac{1}{2} \left\lbrace L_{n}^{\dagger}L_{n}, \rho \right\rbrace \right)
\end{align*}
$$
- Hermitian Hamiltonian for coherent dynamics: ${ H }$ 
- ${ n }$-th dissipator for coupling with bath: ${ L_{n} }$ 

<!-- Hamiltonian -->
Considering quantum Ising model
$$
\begin{align*}
  H 
  &=
  - J \sum_{n = 1}^{L - 1} (1 + \varepsilon_{n}) \sigma_{n}^{z}\sigma_{n + 1}^{z} - \sum_{n = 1}^{L} (h_{x}\sigma_{n}^{x} + h_{z}\sigma_{n}^{z})
\end{align*}
$$
where ${ \varepsilon_{n} }$ is randomly chosen from ${ [ - 0.1, 0.1] }$.

<!-- dissipator -->
Considering the dissipator is 

1. damping
$$
\begin{align*}
  L_{n} = \sqrt{\gamma} \sigma_{n}^{ -}
\end{align*}
$$

2. dephasing
$$
\begin{align*}
  L_{n} = \sqrt{\gamma} \sigma_{n}^{z}
\end{align*}
$$

<!-- traceless -->
In this work, we focus on the traceless non-Hermitian superoperator 
$$
\begin{align*}
  \mathcal{L}
  &=
  \mathcal{L}_\text{origin}  - \frac{\operatorname{tr}\mathcal{L}_\text{origin} }{\operatorname{tr}I} \cdot I
\end{align*}
$$

### B. Non-Hermitian Hamiltonian

<!-- Hamiltonian -->
The Hamiltonian reads
$$
\begin{align*}
  H
  =&
  - \sum_{n = 1}^{L - 1} (1 + \mathrm{i}J \varepsilon_{n}) \sigma_{n}^{z}\sigma_{n + 1}^{z} - \sum_{n = 1}^{L} h( - 2.1\sigma_{n}^{x} + \sigma_{n}^{z}) \\
  &+ 
  \vec{D} \cdot \sum_{n = 1}^{L - 1}  (\vec{\sigma}_{n} \times \vec{\sigma}_{n + 1})
\end{align*}
$$
- on-site random number: ${ \varepsilon_{n} \in [ -1, 1] }$ 
- degree of DM interaction:
$$
\begin{align*}
  \vec{D} = \frac{D}{\sqrt{2}} ( \vec{e}_{x} + \vec{e}_{z})
\end{align*}
$$
DM interaction with given ${ \vec{D} }$ reads
$$
\begin{align*}
  &\frac{D}{\sqrt{2}} (\vec{e}_{x} + \vec{e}_{z}) \cdot \sum_{n = 1}^{L - 1} (\vec{\sigma}_{n} \times \vec{\sigma}_{n + 1}) \\
  &=
  \frac{D}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \sum_{n = 1}^{L - 1} (\vec{\sigma}_{n} \times \vec{\sigma}_{n + 1})^{x} + (\vec{\sigma}_{n} \times \vec{\sigma}_{n + 1})^{z} \right)  \\
  &=
  \frac{D}{\sqrt{2}} \left( \sum_{n = 1}^{L - 1} \sigma_{n}^{y}\sigma_{n + 1}^{z} - \sigma_{n}^{z} \sigma_{n + 1}^{y} + \sigma_{n}^{x}\sigma_{n + 1}^{y}- \sigma_{n}^{y}\sigma_{n + 1}^{z} \right)
\end{align*}
$$

<!-- chaotic properties -->
Such model is non-integrable non-Hermitian, thus the singular values obey random matrix statistics.
